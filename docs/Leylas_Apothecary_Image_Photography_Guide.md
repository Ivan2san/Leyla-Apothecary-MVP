## Photography Style Guide

### Overall Aesthetic

#### Lighting Principles
- **Golden Hour Magic:** Shoot products during sunrise/sunset for warm glow
- **Diffused Natural Light:** Use sheer curtains or north-facing windows
- **Soft Shadows:** Create depth without harshness
- **Candlelight Accents:** For evening/cozy wellness shots
- **Never:** Fluorescent, direct flash, or clinical lighting

#### Composition Rules
- **Rule of Thirds:** Place key elements off-center
- **Negative Space:** 40-60% empty space for calm feeling
- **Natural Arrangements:** Slightly imperfect, organic placement
- **Depth Layers:** Foreground, subject, soft background
- **Leading Lines:** Use herb stems, fabric folds to guide eye

#### Color Grading
```
Lightroom/Photoshop Settings:
- Temperature: +200-400 (warm)
- Tint: +5-10 (slight magenta)
- Exposure: +0.3 to +0.5
- Highlights: -30 to -50
- Shadows: +20 to +40
- Vibrance: -10 to -20 (muted)
- Saturation: -5 to -15
- Clarity: -5 to -10 (soft)
- Dehaze: 0 to +5
- Vignetting: +10 to +20 (subtle)
```

### 2. Product Photography Suite

#### Primary Product Shots (2000x2000px, square)
**Standard Bottle View**
- Background: Pure white or warm off-white
- Angle: Straight on, label fully visible
- Shadow: Soft natural shadow grounding the bottle
- Post: Remove imperfections, enhance amber glow

**Lifestyle Product Shot**
- Setting: Bottle among its source herbs
- Props: Fresh or dried herb sprigs, wooden surface
- Lighting: 45-degree soft natural light
- Composition: Product as hero, herbs supporting

**Scale Reference Shot**
- Include: Hand holding bottle naturally
- Skin tone: Diverse representation across product range
- Nail care: Natural, clean, short nails
- Position: Comfortable grip showing label

**Detail/Macro Shot**
- Focus: Dropper with golden tincture drop
- Background: Completely blurred (bokeh)
- Lighting: Backlit to show liquid transparency
- Timing: Capture mid-drip for dynamism

#### Product Collection Shots
**"Family Photo"**
- Arrangement: 5-7 bottles grouped by use category
- Height variation: Create visual interest with staging
- Props: Subtle herbs between bottles
- Angle: 45-degree elevated view

**"Flat Lay Collection"**
- Surface: Textured linen or raw wood
- Arrangement: Geometric but natural spacing
- Additional: Include dried herbs, vintage spoons
- Lighting: Even, overhead diffused light

## Photography Production Guide

### Equipment Recommendations

#### Camera Settings
```
Aperture: f/2.8 - f/5.6 (soft backgrounds)
Shutter: 1/60 minimum (avoid blur)
ISO: 100-800 (minimal grain)
White Balance: 5200K-5800K (warm)
Format: RAW for maximum editing flexibility
```

#### Essential Props List
- Wooden surfaces (light oak, walnut)
- Linen fabrics (natural, sage, white)
- Ceramic vessels (handmade feel)
- Glass jars (vintage apothecary style)
- Copper/brass implements
- Natural rope/twine
- Aged books/paper
- Fresh and dried herbs
- Crystals/stones (subtle use)
- Candles (beeswax, natural)

### Shooting Schedule Template

#### Monthly Content Calendar
**Week 1: Product Photography**
- New arrivals
- Seasonal restocks
- Bundle creation shots

**Week 2: Lifestyle Content**
- Customer stories
- Wellness routines
- Seasonal activities

**Week 3: Educational Content**
- Process shots
- Ingredient highlights
- How-to demonstrations

**Week 4: Brand/Marketing**
- Email headers
- Social media content
- Promotional materials

### Post-Production Workflow

#### Basic Editing Steps
1. **Import & Organize**
   - Create dated folders
   - Tag by category
   - Rate selects (1-5 stars)

2. **Basic Adjustments**
   - Crop to required dimensions
   - Straighten horizons
   - Remove dust/blemishes

3. **Color Grading**
   - Apply brand preset
   - Fine-tune individual images
   - Ensure consistency across set

4. **Export Settings**
   - Web: JPEG, sRGB, 72-144 DPI
   - Print: JPEG/PDF, Adobe RGB, 300 DPI
   - Social: Platform-specific requirements

### Image Organization System

#### File Naming Convention
```
YYYY-MM-DD_Category_Description_Version
Example: 2024-11-15_Product_Lavender-Tincture_v1
```
